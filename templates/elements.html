What data are we using ?

The dataset provided consists of 61 station points, which measure environmental pollution, temperature, pressure, humidity, and wind. All these data are taken from AQCIN website, which provides free real-time air pollution information for more than 100 countries in the world.

Using the APIs provided from the above website we where able to retrieve data from their database and use it for further analysis.We concentrated our sources in the 5 most polluted cities in Europe, for the moment, which are:

  1. Skopje (6 stations)
  2. Krakow (6 stations)
  3. Belgrade (27 stations)
  4. London (42 stations)
  5. Paris (12 stations).

As further information, we must mention that the air quality is estimated in real time by sensors. We have included data about the following pollutants:

  • PM2.5 particulate matter 2.5 are tiny particles or droplets in the air that are 2.5 microns or less in width. As statistics show, PM2.5 has been estimated to reduce life expenctancy in the Europe by more than eight months.
  • PM10 particulate matter 10 are tiny particles or droplets in the air that are 10 microns or less in width.
  • O3 refers to ground-level ozone. Ground-level ozone comes from pollution emitted from cars, power plants, industrial boilers, refineries, and chemical plants.
  • NO2 refers to nitrogen dioxide, which primarily gets in the air from the burning of fuel (emissions from cars, trucks, power plants etc.)

These pollutants are now generally recognized as the pollutants that in a significant way affect human health. Long-term and peak exposures to these pollutants have a large impact, from impairing the respiratory system to premature death.

  • SO2 refers to sulfur dioxide, which results from the burning of either sulfur or materials containing sulfur.
  • CO refers to carbon monoxide. The main sources of CO to outdoor air are cars and other vehicles or machinery that burn fossil fuels.
