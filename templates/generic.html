Why building an
Air Cloud ?!

In this present scenario, where our planet is being risked by Global Warming, most environment analysis lead us nowhere else but to air pollution problems. Even though many air pollutants have been reduced, these past few years, they remain in high concentrations, making air quality problems persist. This matter doesn’t concern only the environment but consequently the entire global population.

According to WHO : “Air pollution is now considered to be the world’s largest environmental health threat, accounting for 7 million deaths around the world every year. Air pollution causes and exacerbates several diseases, ranging from asthma to cancer, pulmonary illnesses, and heart disease.”

Concentrating more in the European region, statistics according to EEA say that: “Around 90 % of city dwellers in Europe are exposed to pollutants at concentrations higher than the air quality levels deemed harmful to health.” Furthermore, facts put us in front of large parts of the European population, which doesn’t live in what it is considered a “healthy environment”.

Keeping the eyes on these facts, more and more people are trying to contribute with different modelling and monitoring activities. Different organizations are collaborating and collecting financial resources for being able to take real action on this matter, that is extending faster day by day.

These and more news we read and hear every day, are the reason why we decided to build an “Air Cloud”. We are trying to provide to all municipalities, agencies, experts, independent researchers and even students, an Open-Resource for their own analysis and projects, on air pollutants. Consequently, this will lead in making more strategies that with lower the burden of diseases and other catastrophic events attributed to air pollution.
